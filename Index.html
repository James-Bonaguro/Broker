Risk Management

E&O, Privacy & Regulatory Considerations

The regulatory environment is evolving rapidly‚Äî24 states have adopted the NAIC Model Bulletin on AI use as of late 2025.

‚ö†Ô∏è

E&O Exposure

AI-generated quotes may be mispriced. Chatbot errors create liability‚Äîpolicyholders may not know they're chatting with a bot. Courts may hold agents accountable for AI-driven recommendations.

Mitigation: Review ALL AI recommendations before acting. Document processes, recommendations, and outcomes. Run parallel testing until performance validated.
üîí

Data Privacy

California CPRA approved new AI regulations July 2025. 21 states now have comprehensive privacy laws. Risk assessments required every 3 years, retained for 5.

Mitigation: NEVER input client PII into public AI tools. Use enterprise-grade tools with data privacy controls. Document third-party vendor data practices.
üìú

Regulatory Fragmentation

Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205) effective Feb 1, 2026 for "high-risk" AI. Requires risk management policies, annual impact assessments, consumer disclosure before AI decisions.

Mitigation: Implement NIST AI RMF or ISO 42001 for affirmative defense. Notify consumers of AI purpose, decision nature, and appeal process.
ü§ñ

Model Risk (Hallucinations)

AI confidently delivers incorrect outputs. If a chatbot tells a policyholder their homeowners includes flood coverage when it doesn't‚Äîserious claim implications.

Mitigation: Use curated knowledge bases. Require abstention capability. Default to human oversight when uncertain. Consider Armilla AI coverage.
